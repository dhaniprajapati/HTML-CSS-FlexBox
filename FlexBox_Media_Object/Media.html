Chocolate Cosmos

The Chocolate Cosmos (Cosmos atrosanguineus) is a rare and captivating flower known for its deep, velvety dark reddish-brown petals, often appearing almost black. What makes this flower truly unique is its rich chocolate fragrance, which becomes more pronounced in warm weather, making it a favorite among gardeners and flower enthusiasts. Native to Mexico, the Chocolate Cosmos thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, blooming from summer to early fall. It typically grows 40-60 cm (16-24 inches) tall and is often cultivated as a perennial in warm climates or an annual in colder regions. Unlike other cosmos flowers, it does not produce viable seeds, so it is propagated through tubers, similar to dahlias. This flower is not only visually striking but also attracts butterflies and bees, adding life to any garden. With its dramatic color and enchanting chocolate scent, the Chocolate Cosmos is a truly mesmerizing addition to any floral collection.
